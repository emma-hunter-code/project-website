About Me

UK-based Developer in the making.

Code

Becoming a developer was not something I imagined for myself. While I am fairly tech-savvy, I assumed I did not have the correct skills-but then a colleague suggested it could be suited to me, after we collaborated on an IT project. That sparked my curiosity—and I came to realise it was something I loved as I like to design, build and solve problems.

I am currently learning to code with SheCodes, focusing on HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap using VS Code. I manage my code via my GitHub account and host SEO-friendly sites on Netlify. I have leant how to build applications using JavaScript, Python, APIs, and AI tools. My student portfolio reflects what I have learned so far—along with plenty I am still discovering!

I do not have a defined long-term plan just yet, but I really enjoy learning and creating. I can happily tinker for hours.

Work

Since 2009, I have worked in medical research operations, mostly within universities. My role has involved developing processes from the ground up—right through to implementation.

Throughout my career, I have used code in small ways. I work with research data using SPSS and R, and I am now excited to apply Python to similar tasks. I have a professional certificate in Data Analysis from BCS, The Chartered Institute for IT, where I learned how to structure data for business systems including requirements gathering, class diagrams and normalisation.

One of my most rewarding projects involved designing and implementing a system to manage complex research proposals across multiple teams using REDCap. That is where my interest in coding really began—even if I did not realise it at the time.

Design

I am not a professional designer, but I understand the fundamentals of good design and appreciate things that look clean and well-crafted, favouring function over form whilst looking great. I have a degree in Graphic Design at the University for the Creative Arts, which I completed in 2009 (yikes—but still relevant!).

Personal

I am originally from the UK and currently living in Bristol, Southwest England, with my husband and our two cats. In 2023, I was diagnosed with ADHD and a long-term health condition—both of which have shaped how I work and learn. When I am not coding, I enjoy time with my cats, eating out, nail art, shopping, wine, gardening, aromatherapy, and walking.
